Carl Edward Sagan

A lifetime of Discovery



Carl Sagan was a famous American astronomer and popularizer of science. His incredible life was characterized by a burning curiousity about, and passion for, the universe around him. He was one of the most quintessentially human people to have lived in the 20th century, and his loss in 1996 was a loss for all humanity.


    Sagan's Life
  • 1934 Sagan is born in New York City.

  • 1939 Attends the New York World's Fair and is inspired by science.

  • 1954-60 Recieves several degrees, including a PhD from the University of Chicago.

  • 1969-71 Sagan is one of the experts tapped to brief the Apollo astronauts before their historic voyages into space.

  • 1972 Pioneer 10 launches. It is the first spacecraft destined to leave the solar system. Sagan designs a gold anodized plaque with a message from Earth to be attached to the spacecraft.

  • 1974 In conjunction with Frank Drake, Sagan helps design the function that would later come to be known as the Drake Equation. A mathematical equation for calculating the abundance of life in the galaxy.

  • 1980 Along with Lou Friedman and Bruce Murray, Sagan founds the Planetary Society, The world's largest space-advocacy group.

  • 1980 Sagan produces and stars in the revolutionary documentary "Cosmos: A Personal Voyage" which is eventually watched by more than 1 billion people worldwide.

  • 1996 Sagan dies of a rare blood cancer. He is buried in Ithica, New York.
